Instructions


  • The goal of the game is to guess whether the next card drawn will be red or blue. You make your guess by clicking the 'Guess Red' or 'Guess Blue' button. The green lights above the cards track your progress through the game. When you guess correctly a green light will turn on. When you guess incorrectly a green light will turn off. When the final light is turned on you have won the game!


  • The speed bump, the gray bar between the fourth and fifth green lights makes things a bit more interesting... Once you progress past the speed bump, incorrect guesses always take you back to the fourth green light, back across the speed bump. In other words, in order to win the game you need three correct guesses in a row once you have passed the speed bump.


  • Each card in the deck can only be drawn once per shuffle. Each time a card is drawn a little red card or blue card will appear in the 'Played Cards' section - red if the card drawn is red, and blue if the card drawn is blue. This will allow you to employ a bit of strategy in your guesses, based on how many blue or red cards were already drawn. However, if you go through the entire deck without finishing the game, the deck gets reshuffled and all cards are available again. So guess wisely, and good luck!


  • After you win a game all your guesses are tallied and displayed in the window that pops up to congratulate you on your win. After clicking the 'OK' button, or clicking outside of the window, the game will reset and your score will be saved. You can click the 'Your Scores' text above the green lights to view your recent scores. Play again to see if you can win the game with fewer guesses, or play with friends to see who can beat the game with the fewest guesses!


  • At the top of the page, on the left, you can click the 'Back to Portfolio Home' link to go back to my portfolio's home page. You can also click on the Github icon or the Codepen icon to the right of 'View Code:' to take a look at my files and see how I built the game. If you like what you see and want to contact me, please click the 'Contact' link at the bottom of my page next to my name. That link will take you to the contact section of my portfolio where you can view my contact information, or fill out the form - in which case I will get back to you as soon as possible! Thank you for checking out my game! Please feel free to email me (using the contact link to find my email address) with any thoughts, critiques, or questions about this game!
